# Becon Design Specification: The Human Laboratory ## 1. Design Ethos: "Terra & Tide" The visual language for Becon is defined by the **Terra & Tide** design system. It rejects the "sterile tech" aesthetic in favor of a "Humane Laboratory" approach—professional and data-capable, yet warm, trustworthy, and mission-driven. - **Primary Brand Color:** Deep Teal (#1E5F64) - **Secondary/Accents:** Soft Sage Green, Sandstone Neutrals, Earthy Ochre. - **Typography:** Manrope (Clean, modern sans-serif with strong hierarchy). - **Shape Language:** 8px corner radius (Round Eight) for cards and inputs to feel approachable but structured. - **Elevation:** Flat design with very subtle tonal shifts (#f6f4ec backgrounds) and minimal, soft shadows for depth. --- ## 2. Core Layout Architecture (Global App Shell) All portals share a consistent structural foundation to minimize cognitive load while allowing for role-specific density. ### Left Sidebar Navigation - **Header:** Organization logo (Becon) and portal subtitle (e.g., "Donor Portal"). - **Links:** Iconic navigation with clear labels. Active states use a solid brand-color indicator. - **Footer:** Support, Settings, and Log Out actions. ### Top Navigation Bar - **Search:** Centrally or left-aligned contextual search. - **Actions:** Notification bell, help/documentation icon. - **User Identity:** Profile avatar with role context (e.g., "Curator Access", "Super Admin"). ### Main Content Canvas - **Page Header:** Large H1 Title + brief descriptive subtext explaining the page's purpose. - **KPI Strip:** 3-4 high-level metric cards for immediate context. - **Visual Grid:** Cards containing charts, tables, or feed items. --- ## 3. Portal-Specific Design Patterns ### A. Donor Portal (Warm & Editorial) - **Tone:** Reassuring, personal, storytelling-oriented. - **Density:** Low density, generous whitespace. - **Unique Features:** - **Impact Storytelling:** Visual cards connecting dollars to outcomes. - **Selective Analytics:** Simple line and donut charts showing personal giving trends. - **Soft ML:** "Recommended for you" campaign suggestions based on history. ### B. Admin Portal (Operational & Efficient) - **Tone:** Focused, structured, triage-capable. - **Density:** Medium-high density for scanning and action. - **Unique Features:** - **Caseload Workbench:** Complex tables with risk/readiness signals. - **Resident 360:** Deep longitudinal views with timeline components. - **Decision-Support AI:** Integrated risk alerts and "Recommended Next Step" cards. ### C. Super Admin Portal (Strategic & Authoritative) - **Tone:** Strategic, compact, high-trust. - **Density:** High density, executive summary style. - **Unique Features:** - **Governance Matrices:** Complex role-based access control (RBAC) tables. - **ML Control Center:** Technical model monitoring (drift, health, feature importance). - **Audit Trails:** Specialized logs for tracking sensitive system-wide actions. --- ## 4. Reusable Component Library ### KPI Cards - **Values:** Large numeric display with trend percentage (e.g., +12% from last month). - **Visuals:** Subtle background icons or tiny sparklines. ### AI/ML Prediction Modules - **Visual Signature:** Teal/Sage gradient headers or special "AI Assistant" icons. - **Required Elements:** Prediction statement, confidence percentage, top 3 "Reasons why," and a primary CTA (Action). ### Data Tables - **Treatment:** Clean rows with no borders; boundaries defined by subtle background color shifts. - **Interactions:** Row expansion for detail views; action menus for quick edits. ### Charts & Visualizations - **Palette:** Custom "Terra & Tide" hues (Teals, Sages, Neutrals). - **Styles:** Donut (allocation), Line (trends), Stacked Bar (comparison), Heatmap (activity/posting times). --- ## 5. Implementation Guidance for AI When generating new screens or features for Becon: 1. **Always reference the Terra & Tide DESIGN.md.** 2. **Prioritize the "Next Action":** Every screen must answer "What is the most important thing here?" and "What do I do next?". 3. **Contextual AI:** Never hide AI insights in a separate menu; place them where they impact the user's decision (e.g., the Resident Detail page for a caseworker). 4. **Emotional Resonance:** Use "Editorial" headers for Donors and "Operational" headers for Admins.
